1903 Founding
The story of Atletico Madrid begins not in Madrid, but in Bilbao.
1921 Copa Del Rey
Atletico were almost 20 years old by the time they won the regional championship for the first time, and they would go on to win it twice more during the 1920s. Each of these wins qualified them for the national knockout tournament for undisputed supremacy. Dreaming of National supremacy. Twice they came close to winning the national tournament but were defeated the first time by their once-owners Bilbao and the other by separatist flagbearers Barcelona.

Stadium

1936 Civil War
Football became the last thing on Spanish people's minds and lives that same year as fascist forces overthrew the republican (?) government amid claims of corruption. Football competition was suspended as players served in the war or fled to safety. After 2 years of brutal warfare, stadiums across Spain were in ruins and players dead including Atletico's.
1943 Metropolitano reopening
The Metropolitano was finally ready for reopening. By now Atletico had 2 league titles to their name.

Derby

Real Madrid 1959
Having established a home base at the Metropolitano, Atletico set out to tell the rest of Europe that Real are not the only team in Madrid.
Real Madrid 1960
Falling at the last 4 hurdle was a bitter pill to swallow, but the pursuit of recognition within the country borders was not over.
Real Madrid 1961
Real Madrid's stranglehold on the European Cup finally loosened after falling to Barcelona. But Real still won the League. Atletico meanwhile liked the taste of cup glory and set out to retain their trophy.

Europe


1962 Fiorentina
Atletico's 2nd successive Cup final win against Real Madrid qualified them for the UEFA Cup Winners Cup. Fiorentina had won the inaugural UEFA Cup Winners Cup that same year, and the Italians reached the final to defend their title against Atletico at Hampden Park, the scene of Real's last European Cup win.
1974 Bayern
25 years after falling at the semi final stage, Atletico reached their first European Cup final against a Bayern Munich side captained by Franz Beckenbauer.
1975 Independiente
Atletico may have been cursed, but just like in the 1930s they had been handed an unexpected gift. Bayern Munich had declined to participate in the Intercontinental Cup.
1986 Dynamo Kiev
It had been an era of ownership instability, but Vicente Calderon took back the reins to oversee his club to the Parc de Princes, eliminating Celtic along the way again.
